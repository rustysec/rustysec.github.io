Background

I started my professional life in the Military as a US Air Force communications officer. This was a wonderful learning opportunity where I gained invaluable experience leading people, managing projects, and leveraging technical skills.

After an interlude in Afghanistan, I was recruited to join the Air Force Red Team and served as a penetration tester and tool developer.

I ended my active duty career as a strategic operations planner. This was yet another educational endeavour, coordinating and learning along side many cross functional teams. AT this point, however, I decided to transition to a military reservist and attempt to figure out what I wanted to be when I grew up.

Start Up Days

All the way back in 2014, my good friend Chris and I had the brilliant plan to leave our military jobs and start a company. We had some idea of what we were doing, but even that might be overselling it.

Long story short, we managed to convince my very pregnant wife it was a worthy endeavour and we were off to the races.

We experienced all of the start-up-roller-coaster phases you can imagine. Early success, investors, ill-timed industry changing tides, growth, the-opposite-of-growth, pivots, traction, recognition, competition, and finally acquisition in 2022.
